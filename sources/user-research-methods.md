## User Research Methods Reference

This source document provides the methodological foundation for all research activities within this pipeline. It covers method selection, sampling, analysis approaches, and quality standards.

### Method Selection Guide

| Method | Type | Best For | Sample Size | Timeline | Cost |
|--------|------|----------|-------------|----------|------|
| Semi-structured interviews | Qualitative | Exploring motivations, pain points, workflows | 5-12 | 2-3 weeks | Medium |
| Structured interviews | Qualitative | Comparing experiences across participants | 8-15 | 2-3 weeks | Medium |
| Surveys | Quantitative | Measuring attitudes, validating hypotheses | 30-200 | 1-2 weeks | Low |
| Usability testing | Mixed | Evaluating specific interactions | 5-8 | 1-2 weeks | Medium |
| Diary studies | Qualitative | Understanding longitudinal behaviour | 8-15 | 2-4 weeks | High |
| Card sorting | Quantitative | Information architecture, mental models | 15-30 | 1 week | Low |
| A/B testing | Quantitative | Comparing specific design variants | 100+ per variant | 1-4 weeks | Low |
| Contextual inquiry | Qualitative | Understanding real-world work practices | 4-8 | 2-3 weeks | High |

### Mixed Methods Design

When combining qualitative and quantitative methods:

**Sequential exploratory:** Qualitative first (interviews) → use findings to design quantitative study (survey). Use when exploring a new topic.

**Sequential explanatory:** Quantitative first (survey) → use findings to guide qualitative exploration (interviews). Use when you have metrics but do not understand the "why."

**Concurrent:** Run qualitative and quantitative simultaneously. Use when timeline is short and research objectives naturally split across methods.

### Sampling Standards

**Qualitative studies:**
- Purposive sampling: Select participants who can provide the richest data for the research objectives
- Maximum variation sampling: Deliberately include diverse perspectives
- Saturation target: Continue until 2-3 consecutive interviews produce no new themes (typically 8-12 interviews)

**Quantitative studies:**
- Random sampling where possible, stratified sampling when segment comparison is needed
- Minimum sample sizes for statistical significance:
  - Simple frequency analysis: 30 respondents
  - Segment comparison (2 groups): 30 per segment
  - Correlation analysis: 50 respondents
  - Factor analysis: 100+ respondents

### Thematic Analysis Framework

This pipeline uses a six-phase thematic analysis approach:

1. **Familiarisation:** Read all data before coding
2. **Initial coding:** Generate codes from the data (not from a pre-determined framework)
3. **Theme search:** Group codes into candidate themes
4. **Theme review:** Check themes against the data set — do they hold up?
5. **Theme definition:** Name and define each theme precisely
6. **Report production:** Write up findings with evidence

### Quality Criteria

**For qualitative research:**
- Credibility: Findings are believable (achieved through triangulation, member checking, thick description)
- Transferability: Findings can be applied to other contexts (achieved through rich contextual description)
- Dependability: Findings are consistent (achieved through audit trail)
- Confirmability: Findings are shaped by participants, not researcher bias (achieved through reflexivity)

**For quantitative research:**
- Validity: The survey measures what it claims to measure
- Reliability: Results would be consistent if the survey were administered again
- Response rate: Minimum 20% for external surveys, 40% for internal surveys
- Completion rate: At least 80% of those who start should finish

### Ethical Standards

All research conducted through this pipeline must adhere to:
- Informed consent: Participants understand what they are agreeing to
- Voluntary participation: Participants can withdraw at any time without consequence
- Confidentiality: Personal data is protected and findings are anonymised
- Data minimisation: Collect only what is needed for the research objectives
- Secure storage: Data is stored securely and deleted when no longer needed
- No deception: Participants are told the true purpose of the research
